Opioid Crisis in the United States (2019)

 

Overview

Millions of Americans suffer from pain and are often prescribed opioids to treat their conditions. However, the dangers of prescription misuse, opioid use disorder, and overdose have been a growing problem throughout the United States.Drug Overdose remains a major concern in United States. In 2019, 70,630 drug overdose deaths occurred in the United States. Opioids currently are the main driver of drug overdose deaths. This includes prescription opioids, synthetic opioids and heroin.

The purpose of this visualization is to explore the following question: Which regions/states have been impacted the most by the opioid crisis/overdose deaths in the US during 2019?

Note: Data for the visualizations are from CDC.org primarily for the year 2019. User's can navigate through the slideshow by clicking the arrows on the left/right sides of the screen, clicking the navigation dots on top, or using the left/right buttons on a keyboard. The decription for the graphs are provided in the section below the graph and can be seen by scrolling down/up.

Summary

Narrative Structure The visualization follows an interactive slideshow that guides(author led) the user through various scenes. The introduction slides set the context around the nature of data and the introduction to the narrative visualization. In the main set of scenes, the first scene shows the region wise breakdown across the country(US) for the average number of deaths due to drug overdose in year 2019. There is an annotation in this scene that points to the region that has the highest number of deaths i.e North East The second slide shows a drill down and continuation from scene1 to get into the states for the top most region(i.e. NorthEast US) that contribute to the highest deaths in US. Similar to scene1 this scene also provides an annotation to highlight the State thats having the worst death rate compared to the rest in 2019. and the third slide gives the user ability(user-led scene) to interactively check /uncheck checkboxes for the region so that the user can understand the death rate acorss all states in US filterd by region.

Scenes The first main slide with chart showcases the average deaths across various regions in US for 2019 showing an overview of the Drug Overdose within the United States.

Annotations Slide 1 with chart also has an annotation that is meant to draw the user's attention to the area of the chart that highlights the region impacted by the drug overdose problem. Slide 2 with chart provides a drill down into the states within the top most region "North East -US" with an annotation to highlight the state("NY") with highest average deaths. This provides continuation from previous chart where the user is author-led to drill down to the states corresponding to the top region with deaths due to opioids.

Parameters Slide3 with Chart includes parameters (filters for region) that allow users to interact with the data via filtering. The scenes aims to answer a simple question and these parameters allow users explore data in more detail. For example, California, Texas, Florida and Northeast states seem to have much more drug overdose deaths than other states. When a particular region is selected, the scene changes to show the data for the corresponding states in the region.

Triggers Choosing different filters on charts 3 triggers changes to the parameters discussed above triggers the charts with a different state level data corresponding to the filtered region to reflect the changes and show the relevant information just pertaining to the filters selected.
